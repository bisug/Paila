# MAIN CHALLENGE

**Seamless Nepal: Bridging the 1st & Last Mile Gap with Community at the Center**

## Problem
Tourists often face inconsistent and difficult experiences from arrival to departure — especially in emerging destinations and trekking areas outside Kathmandu and Pokhara. At the same time, local homestays, guides, artisans, and villages receive limited direct benefits from tourism.

## The Challenge
Build a mobile app, web platform, AI tool, chatbot, or digital system that delivers a smooth and trustworthy journey for tourists while directly empowering local communities to earn more, participate actively, and preserve their culture.

This challenge is open to Grade 11 students to undergraduates from all backgrounds — including tech, design, hospitality, tourism, arts, social sciences, business, and non-technical fields.

## What to Build
You can create:
- Mobile app prototype (Flutter, React Native, MIT App Inventor, Glide, etc.)
- Web platform or dashboard
- AI-powered travel assistant / chatbot
- UI/UX heavy Figma prototype + user journey
- Gamified experience or AR filter
- No-code / low-code solutions are welcome

*No advanced coding experience required. Strong ideas, cultural understanding, and good design are highly valued.*

## Key Features to Include (Choose 3–5)
- Smart discovery and booking of homestays, local guides, and experiences
- Trust & safety layer (verified profiles, ratings, emergency support)
- Transparent pricing and fair revenue sharing for locals
- Simple digital identity / certification for homestays and guides
- Multi-language support (Nepali + English + others)
- Sustainability tools (eco-score, responsible travel nudges, off-season incentives)
- Special support for women and youth-led businesses

### Bonus Impact Areas
- Offline functionality for remote areas
- Features that clearly create jobs or income for women, youth & marginalized groups
- Integration ideas with NTB or local tourism services
- Living heritage & cultural storytelling elements

## Alignment
This challenge directly supports the UNDP – NTB Sustainable Tourism Project, focusing on:
- Digital transformation of tourism
- Community livelihoods and inclusive employment
- Sustainable destination development
- Heritage preservation

## Judging Criteria
- Innovation & Creativity
- Feasibility in Nepali context (offline, low-data, affordable)
- User Experience & Cultural Sensitivity
- Community Impact & Inclusivity (especially women & youth)
- Prototype Quality & Presentation
